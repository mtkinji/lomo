# Elena: Recover When I Drift

## Audience / Persona

Audience: `audience-life-transition-restarters`  
Persona: Elena

Elena had intentions, then life interrupted them. She needs to return without shame and choose what still fits.

## Hero JTBD

`jtbd-recover-when-i-drift-from-an-arc` - Help me recover when I drift from an Arc.

## Job Flow

1. Realize she has drifted.
2. Understand what changed.
3. Decide what still matters.
4. Let go of what no longer fits.
5. Choose one small next step.
6. Rebuild trust with the system.
7. Reflect on the season without shame.

## Current Kwilt Flow

1. Goals and Activities can show what stalled.
2. Notifications can nudge, if configured gently.
3. Chapters can summarize the season.
4. Goal/Arc lifecycle work can support pausing or retiring.
5. Trust and calm UX rules prevent punitive framing.

## Offerings

- Drift-aware JTBD and tone rules.
- Notifications and reminders.
- Chapters as retrospective sensemaking.
- Goal lifecycle and limits.
- Activity history.

## Delivery Score

| Step | Score | Rationale |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Realize drift | 2 | Drift can be inferred, but not yet handled as a dedicated flow. |
| Understand change | 3 | Chapters can help, but may not directly explain drift. |
| Decide what matters | 3 | Goals and Arcs help; recommitment flow needs care. |
| Let go | 2 | Pause/retire affordances are not yet the emotional center. |
| Choose next step | 3 | Plan/recommendations can help, but return-specific guidance is thin. |
| Rebuild trust | 3 | Philosophy is strong; product proof depends on tone and control. |
| Reflect without shame | 4 | Chapters and primer align well with this need. |

## Gaps

- Elena needs an explicit return path, not just stale goals and reminders.
- Kwilt should help distinguish "still mine" from "season changed."
- Drift recovery should create relief before it creates action.

## Aspirational Design Challenge

How might we help Elena return to what still matters after drift, without making the gap feel like failure?
