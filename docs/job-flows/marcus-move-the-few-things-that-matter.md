# Marcus: Move The Few Things That Matter

## Audience / Persona

Audience: `audience-burned-out-productivity-power-users`  
Persona: Marcus

Marcus has enough tools. His job is not to track more work; it is to decide what matters, move it, and trust the system enough not to rebuild it next week.

## Hero JTBD

`jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - Help me make real progress in the few areas I most want to grow.

## Job Flow

1. Notice that the current system is too noisy.
2. Name the few commitments that matter this season.
3. Turn one commitment into a concrete goal.
4. Break the goal into activities.
5. Decide what to do next.
6. Capture progress without maintaining the system.
7. Review whether the work is still worth doing.

## Current Kwilt Flow

1. Goals tab and limits push toward a small active set.
2. Arc and Goal creation capture why the goal matters.
3. To-dos/Activities hold concrete work.
4. Plan helps schedule or sequence next action.
5. Chapters and recaps can reflect on what happened.
6. Desktop and AI operator work are planned to make denser organization easier.

## Offerings

- Arcs as identity direction.
- Goals as the few active commitments.
- Activities as the plan in motion.
- Plan surface and recommendations.
- Chapters for retrospective sensemaking.
- Desktop command center and AI operator work.

## Delivery Score

| Step | Score | Rationale |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Notice noise | 2 | Kwilt avoids productivity clutter, but does not yet diagnose system overload. |
| Name few commitments | 3 | Goals and limits support focus; prioritization help is still emerging. |
| Turn commitment into goal | 4 | Goal creation and Arc linkage are core strengths. |
| Break into activities | 3 | Activities exist, but planning assistance can become stronger. |
| Decide next action | 3 | Plan and recommendations help, but the "what now?" moment is not yet the spine. |
| Capture progress | 4 | Capture-first posture is strong. |
| Review worth | 3 | Chapters help, but this review loop is not fully connected to goal pruning. |

## Gaps

- Marcus needs stronger "what now?" decision relief.
- The app should help prune or pause commitments, not just create them.
- AI help must reduce maintenance, not create another planning layer.

## Aspirational Design Challenge

How might we help Marcus keep only the commitments worth carrying and know the next honest action, without turning Kwilt into another productivity system to maintain?
