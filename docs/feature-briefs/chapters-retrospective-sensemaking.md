# Chapters Retrospective Sensemaking

## Context

Chapters are the shipped retrospective layer in Kwilt. They help a user look back on a season, notice what actually got attention, and return to what still matters without turning drift into a failure state.

## Target audience

`audience-life-transition-restarters` represents people who had intentions, then life interrupted them. Chapters matter because this audience needs the app to help them make sense of what happened before it asks them to make a new plan.

## Representative persona

Elena is returning after a hard or crowded season. She does not need a shame prompt or a performance review; she needs a clear lookback that helps her decide what is still worth carrying.

## Aspirational design challenge

How might we help Elena understand the season she just lived through and return to what still matters, while preserving Kwilt's non-shaming drift-recovery posture?

## Hero JTBD

`jtbd-recover-when-i-drift-from-an-arc` — Chapters support Elena's return by helping her see the gap, the pattern, and the remaining signal without treating drift as a moral failure.

## Job flow step

`job-flow-elena-recover-when-i-drift` identifies "Understand what changed" and "Reflect on the season without shame" as key steps. Chapters are the strongest current Kwilt offering for those steps because they turn completed and stalled work into a retrospective narrative.

## JTBD framing

When Elena comes back to Kwilt after life has moved around her, she needs to understand what happened and decide what still matters. Chapters serve `jtbd-make-sense-of-the-season` and `jtbd-see-who-im-becoming` by showing the lived pattern, and they serve `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life` by keeping the recap honest, calm, and user-controlled.

## Design

The Chapters feature includes a chapter inventory, a detail canvas for retrospective reading, an alignment surface for reconnecting chapter meaning to Arcs, digest settings, and lightweight snippets that can surface reflection elsewhere in the app.

Chapters are always lookbacks. They should report what happened, where attention accumulated, what mostly waited, and what changed. They should not become future-planning containers or AI-written motivational speeches.

The public docs translation should explain Chapters as "reflection without homework": a way to see what moved, what waited, and what might be worth carrying next.

## Success signal

Elena can read a Chapter and name one true thing about the season without feeling judged by the app. The chapter should make the next honest choice easier, whether that is recommitting, shrinking, pausing, or letting something go.

## Open questions

- How much of Chapter generation is user-triggered versus cadence-based digest behavior?
- Which Chapter insights should flow back into Arc and Goal surfaces without turning Chapters into planning objects?
