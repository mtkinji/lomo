# Home Today Orientation

## Context

The Home/Today surface is the user's calm re-entry point into Kwilt. It should orient the user toward what matters now without becoming a dashboard, feed, or second navigation system.

## Target audience

`audience-faith-and-values-driven-builders` needs a day-facing surface that connects ordinary action back to identity direction. Home matters because Sarah should not have to reconstruct her life system every time she opens the app.

## Representative persona

Sarah opens Kwilt with limited attention. She wants to see what belongs to today and why it matters, then move one useful thing without losing the larger thread of who she is becoming.

## Aspirational design challenge

How might we help Sarah re-enter Kwilt through today's most relevant choices, while preserving the app shell/canvas split and avoiding dashboard clutter?

## Hero JTBD

`jtbd-see-who-im-becoming` — Home supports Sarah by making today's activity feel connected to a direction of becoming instead of a disconnected task list.

## Job flow step

`job-flow-sarah-see-who-im-becoming` names "Capture ordinary actions that belong to the Arc" and "Notice progress without reducing it to metrics" as key steps. Home should make those steps easier by surfacing current relevance inside the app canvas.

## JTBD framing

When Sarah opens Kwilt, she needs a place that says, "Here is what matters today, and here is why it belongs." The Today surface serves `jtbd-see-my-arcs-in-everyday-moments` by connecting ordinary action to Arcs, while also serving `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` and `jtbd-capture-and-find-meaning` through practical next actions.

## Design

Home is a canvas, not a shell. Primary navigation remains outside the canvas; the Today surface presents current relevance: actions, context, and cues that help the user pick the next useful move.

The surface should stay quiet. It should reduce re-entry friction, not add a management layer. Strong Home content points to Arcs, Goals, Activities, and Chapters without trying to replace their dedicated canvases.

The public docs translation should explain Home/Today as the place to re-enter: what to look for, what it means when something appears there, and how to move from today into the deeper object surfaces.

## Success signal

Sarah can open Kwilt and choose one useful next action without hunting across the app or feeling like she needs to maintain another dashboard.

## Open questions

- Which Today elements should appear only when there is a strong signal versus as default empty-state guidance?
- How much Chapter or recap content belongs on Home before it competes with the dedicated Chapters canvas?
