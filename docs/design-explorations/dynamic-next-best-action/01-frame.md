# Frame: Dynamic Next Best Action

## What the user said
> Replace capability-based actions with a recommendation-based action system. Kwilt should answer: "What's the most useful thing to do next?"

## Restated in user voice
When I am looking at a to-do and am not sure whether to schedule it, work on it, break it down, ask for help, or finish it, I want Kwilt to recommend the next honest move, so that I can make progress without maintaining another productivity system.

## Target audience
`audience-burned-out-productivity-power-users`: Burned-out productivity power users who already have enough tools and need decision relief.

## Representative persona
Marcus: Marcus wants fewer active commitments and more actual movement. He is wary of surfaces that ask him to configure a system before he can act.

- Current situation: Marcus is on an Activity Detail screen deciding what to do next.
- What they're trying to become/do: Move one meaningful commitment forward.
- Emotional state or tension: Slightly overloaded by choice; open to guidance if it does not feel like another dashboard.
- What would make this feel wrong to them: A generic AI chat destination, productivity-app urgency, or hidden actions that feel removed.

## Hero anchor
`jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - Help me make real progress in the few areas I most want to grow.

## Job flow step
Marcus job flow step 5, "Decide what to do next," is scored 3. Kwilt has Plan and recommendations, but the "what now?" moment is not yet the spine.

## Active anchors
- `jtbd-carry-intentions-into-action` - The button should help translate a saved intention into the next concrete move.
- `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - The recommender reduces tool-choice overhead around important work.
- `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life` - Keeping alternatives discoverable protects trust while changing the primary surface.

## Friction we're addressing
The current Activity Detail dock asks the user to pick a feature first. AI help is also framed as a destination, which requires Marcus to know what to ask before Kwilt helps him.

## Aspirational design challenge
How might we help Marcus decide the next honest action for a to-do, while preserving Kwilt's calm capture-first posture and keeping alternatives discoverable?

## Out of scope
Personalized ranking, behavior-pattern learning, new AI prompt flows, and cross-screen recommendation infrastructure.

## Open question
Should future recommendations explain why an action was chosen, or stay quiet unless the user asks?
