# Frame: Meaningful First App Access

## What the user said
> Explore the next version of Screen Time protection: a user should not be able to use less productive apps like YouTube or Instagram until they have done some qualifying action in Kwilt. Duolingo has this now, and it feels like a nice way to keep from doing less productive things.

## Restated in user voice
When I reach for apps that usually pull me away, I want my phone to nudge me back toward one meaningful Kwilt action first, so that the day begins with real movement instead of drift.

## Target audience
`audience-burned-out-productivity-power-users`: Burned-out productivity power users who know their distraction patterns and want guardrails that reduce maintenance instead of becoming another system.

## Representative persona
Marcus: Marcus is capable and motivated, but his attention can be captured before his intentions have a chance to become action.

- Current situation: Marcus opens a less productive app before doing the one thing he meant to move today.
- What they're trying to become/do: Become someone whose first move reflects the commitments he actually cares about.
- Emotional state or tension: He wants friction in the right place, but he will reject anything that feels patronizing, gamified, or hard to undo.
- What would make this feel wrong to them: A scolding shield, a fake "engagement" task, irreversible blocking, or a streak mechanic dressed up as self-control.

## Hero anchor
`jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - Help me make real progress in the few areas I most want to grow.

## Job flow step
Marcus job flow step 1, "Notice that the current system is too noisy," is scored 2. Kwilt avoids productivity clutter, but does not yet help when the user's broader phone environment pulls them away. The feature also strengthens step 5, "Decide what to do next," by making a meaningful next action the path back to app access.

## Active anchors
- `jtbd-carry-intentions-into-action` - The guardrail turns a fragile intention into a required first move before distraction.
- `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - The qualifying action must be tied to real movement on Activities, Goals, or Focus Sessions.
- `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life` - Device-level gating requires opt-in setup, clear status, reliable unlock, and a non-shaming override.

## Friction we're addressing
Distraction often wins before the user has made an intentional choice. Traditional blockers create a separate restriction system to maintain. Kwilt can make the better move easier by asking for one meaningful action before selected apps open.

## Aspirational design challenge
How might we help Marcus put one meaningful Kwilt action before distracting apps, while preserving user agency, calm language, and trust that the restriction is optional and reversible?

## Out of scope
Punitive locks, public accountability, streak pressure, app-usage shaming, child/parent control flows, multiple complex profiles, and blocking capture or emergency phone use.

## Open question
Should V1 unlock selected apps for the rest of the day after one qualifying action, or unlock a shorter user-chosen access window?
