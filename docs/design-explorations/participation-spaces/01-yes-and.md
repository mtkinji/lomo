# Yes-And Expansion: Participation Spaces

## Original idea

People can belong to several friendly, named Spaces, while other people may enter an existing Space with narrowly defined rights; personal Kwilt surfaces still aggregate across those Spaces.

## Adjacencies

### Yes, and what if Spaces let capability teams reuse one understandable participation contract?

- Serves: `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`, `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`
- Job elevation: from relearning different sharing rules in every capability to understanding a consistent invitation and access promise.
- New value: Screen Time, Money, Goals, Recipes, Stories, Meals, and Activities can declare their own grants without inventing unrelated identity systems.
- Cost delta: high.
- Anti-pattern check: pass only if capability language stays specific; a generic permissions console would fail.

### Yes, and what if one personal action layer made multi-Space participation feel simpler than workspace software?

- Serves: `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter`, `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`
- Job elevation: from checking several contexts to knowing what is mine to do now.
- New value: My To-dos, Today, Plan, Search, Notifications, and Agent become responsibility-aware projections across every authorized source.
- Cost delta: medium to high.
- Anti-pattern check: pass; a current-Space switcher is an instant failure.

### Yes, and what if a one-off share could stay one-off?

- Serves: `jtbd-capture-and-find-meaning`, `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`
- Job elevation: from administering a group to simply giving someone something useful.
- New value: send a recipe copy, story, list, or plan without creating a roster, durable Space, or paid seat.
- Cost delta: medium.
- Anti-pattern check: pass if copy versus live sharing is explicit and provenance survives.

### Yes, and what if repeated sharing could gently become a named Space later?

- Serves: `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`, `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`
- Job elevation: from repeatedly choosing the same people to recognizing a durable family context only after demand appears.
- New value: repeated recipe or story sharing can become **Watanabe Family** without front-loading family-tree setup.
- Cost delta: medium.
- Anti-pattern check: pass if the suggestion is optional and never blocks the share.

### Yes, and what if the same person could participate differently in different Spaces?

- New value: Grandma can be a recipe contributor in one Space, child-specific caregiver in another, and owner of her own personal Space.

### Yes, and what if support did not require co-ownership?

### Yes, and what if sponsorship paid for useful participation without buying access to data?

- Serves: `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`, `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`
- Job elevation: from confusing who pays with who belongs to making family access generous and privacy-safe.

- Cost delta: high.
- Anti-pattern check: pass only if downgrade and sponsor-loss behavior never destroys personal data or changes grants silently.

### Yes, and what if Spaces made family lifecycle changes survivable?

- Serves: `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`
- Job elevation: from a static family roster to relationships that can change without data loss or coercion.
- New value: co-parenting, a child aging into an independent account, elder care, role transfer, separation, death, and loss of sponsorship can have explicit, humane transitions.
- Cost delta: high.
- Anti-pattern check: pass if the system avoids irreversible ownership traps and punitive exit.

## Candidate missing demand

The expansion reveals two needs not clearly represented in the current taxonomy:

1. **Carry family knowledge across households and generations.** This may deserve a feature-level JTBD under `jtbd-capture-and-find-meaning` if recipe/story use proves recurring.
2. **See my own responsibilities across every context in which I participate.** This currently appears as a missing job step in Maya's and potentially Marcus's flows rather than a new top-level JTBD.

Payment, role administration, and Space creation are not themselves candidate JTBDs. They are supply-side mechanisms or lifecycle steps serving privacy, participation, and trust.

## Frame recommendation

**Run the design-thinking loop with the expanded frame.**

The original Screen Time/Household frame is too narrow to own these primitives. The exploration should frame Spaces as a cross-capability participation system, while keeping the family Screen Time program as one bounded consumer and refusing to build a generic collaboration product before the jobs and rubric converge.
