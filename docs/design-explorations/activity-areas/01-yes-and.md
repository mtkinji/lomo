# Yes-And: Activity Areas

## Expansion

The offered idea is not just a field on to-dos. It can become the bridge between Activity capture, scheduling setup, and contextual recommendations.

1. **Yes, and Areas can replace rough scheduling domains.**
   The current `work` vs `personal` scheduling mode is useful but too narrow. Areas can provide the durable user-owned concept, while scheduling keeps a compatibility mapping such as `area.availabilityMode`.

2. **Yes, and Settings can make Areas feel owned, not inferred.**
   A user-managed list with intelligent defaults gives Maya control without requiring up-front setup. The list belongs in Settings because it defines how Kwilt understands her life over time.

3. **Yes, and scheduling setup becomes more human.**
   Instead of "configure availability rules," Kwilt can ask, "When do these areas usually fit?" Work, Family, Home, Health, and Personal can each have soft time windows.

4. **Yes, and Area can improve Recommended without adding a mode selector.**
   If the current moment or surface suggests a work window, work Area tasks can rise. Personal tasks should not disappear; they should stop competing so loudly.

5. **Yes, and Area can help AI explain itself.**
   "Suggested for your Work area" or "Usually fits in Family time" is easier to trust than opaque scheduling.

6. **Yes, and Area can become an inspectable correction path.**
   If Kwilt schedules something wrong, changing its Area is a calm correction that teaches future scheduling.

7. **Yes, and defaults can be personalized later.**
   V1 can seed `Work`, `Personal`, `Family`, `Home`, and `Health`. Later, onboarding or AI can suggest adding `Church`, `School`, `Side project`, or other user-specific Areas.

## Job Elevation

The job is not "categorize to-dos." The elevated job is: help the user put commitments into the part of life where they can actually move.

## Frame Recommendation

Run the loop with an expanded frame: **Activity Areas as a Settings-managed life-domain system that feeds scheduling and recommendations**.
