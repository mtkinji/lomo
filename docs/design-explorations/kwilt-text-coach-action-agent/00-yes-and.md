# Yes-And: kwilt-text-coach-action-agent

## Original idea (restated)

Bring the Kwilt-keep SMS-first coach concept into Kwilt as a native Pro surface: a text-based coach/action agent that can capture, follow up, and perform bounded actions for the user outside the app.

## Yes, and what if it could...

**Yes, and what if it could...** become the missing "between intention and follow-through" layer for Kwilt.

- Serves: `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter`
- Job elevation: moves beyond planning or suggestions into helping intentions survive the gap between noticing and doing.
- New value: Pro becomes "Kwilt follows through with me," not "Kwilt gives me more AI."
- Cost delta vs. original: medium
- Anti-pattern check: pass if actions are bounded, auditable, and never framed as productivity optimization.

**Yes, and what if it could...** make capture happen in the most natural channel the user already uses: texting.

- Serves: `jtbd-capture-and-find-meaning`
- Job elevation: the user can text Kwilt the moment an intention appears, with no app open and no object-selection ceremony.
- New value: capture becomes a conversation, not an app session.
- Cost delta vs. original: medium
- Anti-pattern check: pass if capture remains free/generous and never requires Arc/Goal selection.

**Yes, and what if it could...** close loops through minimal replies like "done," "snooze," "pause," and "not relevant."

- Serves: `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter`
- Job elevation: follow-through no longer depends on the user maintaining a list; the system asks at the right moment and records the outcome.
- New value: creates paid WOW moments from real completion evidence, not dashboards.
- Cost delta vs. original: medium
- Anti-pattern check: pass if prompts are calm, capped, and easy to pause.

**Yes, and what if it could...** draft messages to other people without sending them autonomously.

- Serves: `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`, `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`
- Job elevation: reduces activation energy for relational action while keeping the user as the sender and author.
- New value: strong monetizable moments: apology drafts, check-in texts, birthday cards, support offers.
- Cost delta vs. original: medium
- Anti-pattern check: pass only if "draft-first" is the default and auto-send is explicitly out of scope.

**Yes, and what if it could...** connect relational follow-through to Kwilt's Arc/Goal/Activity model.

- Serves: `jtbd-see-my-arcs-in-everyday-moments`, `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter`
- Job elevation: "call Dad," "bring a meal," or "send the apology" becomes evidence of who the user is becoming, not a standalone CRM task.
- New value: Kwilt-keep's relational memory becomes identity-linked Activity evidence inside Kwilt.
- Cost delta vs. original: high
- Anti-pattern check: pass if anchoring remains optional and confirmation-based.

**Yes, and what if it could...** become the first real user-facing "agent permission" system.

- Serves: `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`
- Job elevation: the user can grant narrow standing permissions, starting with low-risk Activity actions and loop-closure prompts.
- New value: creates a durable Pro platform for action agents without starting with risky external actions.
- Cost delta vs. original: high
- Anti-pattern check: pass if permissions are legible, reversible, and action logs are visible.

**Yes, and what if it could...** support family/household use later without making the first Kwilt-native version social by default.

- Serves: `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`
- Job elevation: the eventual shared-memory surface helps users coordinate care without exposure, but the first version can stay one-user, private-by-default.
- New value: preserves Kwilt-keep's household wedge as a future expansion.
- Cost delta vs. original: high
- Anti-pattern check: pass if no default-public sharing, no feeds, and no surveillance/contact scraping.

## Candidate-missing anchors

- **Help me carry intentions into trusted follow-through without having to manage every step myself.** Surfaced by the agent/action adjacencies. The current `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` captures real progress, but not the specific demand for a trusted system to carry intention across time, channels, prompts, drafts, and loop closure. Recommend adding as a mid-level sub-job under `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter`.

## Frame recommendation

**Run design-thinking-loop with an expanded frame**: not "text-based coach" as a channel feature, but "Kwilt-native follow-through agent that uses texting as the first high-WOW surface for capture, prompt, action help, and loop closure."
