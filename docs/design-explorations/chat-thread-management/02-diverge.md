# Diverge: Chat Thread Management

Axis: action density versus safety.

## A. Bidirectional swipe
Swipe right for Archive and left for Delete. It is compact, follows the existing Kwilt swipe grammar, and gives the reversible and destructive actions distinct directions. Best when cleanup should stay in context; fails if destructive deletion is allowed without confirmation.

## B. One-sided action tray
Swipe left to reveal both Archive and Delete. It makes both actions discoverable together but creates a wider, denser tray and increases mis-tap risk in the narrow menu.

## C. Long-press menu
Long-press a chat to open Archive and Delete. It is safe and technically simple, but hidden and does not answer the requested swipe behavior.

All three preserve the four-object model and capture-first behavior because they manage only Chat continuity. None adds productivity pressure, scoring, dashboards, or anthropomorphic language.
