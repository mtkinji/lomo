# Frame: Focus Protection

## What the user said
> Focus Protection helps users reduce distractions during focus sessions by applying optional device-level protections such as Screen Time restrictions, notification suppression, and future focus-mode integrations. Users only ever start a normal Focus Session.

## Restated in user voice
When I am ready to work on something that matters, I want Kwilt to help quiet the usual digital distractions automatically, so that starting a Focus Session feels like entering a steadier work environment instead of configuring another productivity system.

## Target audience
`audience-burned-out-productivity-power-users`: Burned-out productivity power users who have tried many systems and are wary of tools that create more maintenance.

## Representative persona
Marcus: Marcus has a concrete Activity in front of him and wants to move the few commitments that matter. He is open to lightweight protection if it helps him follow through without turning Kwilt into another distraction-blocking app to manage.

- Current situation: Marcus starts a Focus Session for an Activity, but social apps, notifications, and habitual switching can still pull him away.
- What they're trying to become/do: Become someone who can return attention to meaningful commitments without constant system upkeep.
- Emotional state or tension: Motivated but fragile; he wants help that feels quiet and practical.
- What would make this feel wrong to them: A second "protected focus" workflow, complex profiles, gamified self-control, or opaque device controls that are hard to undo.

## Hero anchor
`jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - Help me make real progress in the few areas I most want to grow.

## Job flow step
Marcus job flow step 5, "Decide what to do next," is scored 3: Plan and recommendations help, but the "what now?" moment is not yet the spine. Focus Protection also strengthens step 6, "Capture progress without maintaining the system," because protection should activate and clean up without repeated setup.

## Active anchors
- `jtbd-carry-intentions-into-action` - Protection helps a chosen intention survive the vulnerable transition from "I should focus" to actual follow-through.
- `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - The feature makes meaningful work easier to begin and complete.
- `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life` - Device-level controls require transparent permissions, clean cleanup, and a reliable manual off path.

## Friction we're addressing
Focus mode currently helps the user commit time to an Activity, but it cannot protect the surrounding device environment. Dedicated distraction blockers often solve that by making the user manage profiles and rules. Kwilt's opportunity is to make protection an optional Focus setting that disappears during normal use.

## Aspirational design challenge
How might we help Marcus enter a quieter work environment when he starts a Focus Session, while preserving one Focus concept, configure-once simplicity, and trust that protections will always clear?

## Out of scope
Multiple protection profiles, task-specific protection settings, Goal-specific rules, Arc-specific rules, gamification, streaks, and a separate protected-focus workflow.

## Open question
Does V1 require a custom shield screen/action extension, or can the MVP ship with system-default Screen Time shielding plus strong cleanup and Settings visibility?
