# Yes-And: Family Screen Time

## Original idea

Kwilt runs on caregiver and child devices so caregivers can govern Screen Time rules and child devices can enforce them through Apple's Screen Time APIs.

## Research-informed adjacencies

**Yes, and what if it could resolve ordinary access without asking a caregiver?**

- Serves: `jtbd-carry-intentions-into-action`
- Job elevation: Turn agreed family expectations into dependable follow-through without a repeated human handoff.
- New value: Conditions can move from review-required to automatic once the family trusts the loop.
- Cost delta vs. original: medium
- Anti-pattern check: pass if automation is deterministic, scoped, visible, and reversible; failure if AI judges deservingness.

**Yes, and what if it could distinguish baseline access, required-before-access conditions, and optional ways to earn more?**

- Serves: `jtbd-put-intention-before-impulse`
- Job elevation: Protect important family rhythms without pricing every ordinary responsibility in minutes.
- New value: Families can say “homework before games” without saying all leisure must be purchased through chores.
- Cost delta vs. original: medium
- Anti-pattern check: pass; avoids a chore marketplace and punitive scorekeeping.

**Yes, and what if children could intentionally start a usage-based access session?**

- Serves: `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`
- Job elevation: Make access and expiration predictable instead of silently draining a wall-clock allowance.
- New value: The child chooses **Games for 20 minutes**, sees the remaining time, and knows what happens at zero.
- Cost delta vs. original: high
- Anti-pattern check: pass if the surface is calm and does not gamify consumption.

**Yes, and what if independence could grow one agreement at a time?**

- Serves: `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`
- Job elevation: Move from parent-enforced compliance toward bounded self-management.
- New value: One recurring condition can become auto-approved without giving the child broad account authority.
- Cost delta vs. original: medium
- Anti-pattern check: pass; no global child score, streak, or identity judgment.

**Yes, and what if both caregivers could govern the same children with independent identities?**

- Serves: `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`
- Job elevation: Make family responsibility truly shared without shared passwords or one permanent gatekeeper.
- New value: Either caregiver can answer; the first decision closes the request for both; history records who acted.
- Cost delta vs. original: high
- Anti-pattern check: pass if Screen Time authority does not expose unrelated private Kwilt data.

**Yes, and what if approval included proof that the child device applied it?**

- Serves: `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`
- Job elevation: Replace hopeful remote control with trustworthy delivery.
- New value: Caregivers see **Applied on Riley's iPad**, **Device offline**, or **Shield restored**, while local expiry remains reliable offline.
- Cost delta vs. original: high
- Anti-pattern check: pass; this is calm operational truth, not a performance dashboard.

**Yes, and what if Screen Time established Kwilt's private household participation layer?**

- Serves: `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`
- Job elevation: Give operational family capabilities a durable home without flattening them into broader-family sharing.
- New value: The same household identity and scoped authority can later serve Chores, Money, Meals, and family Goals while keeping each capability's data private by default.
- Cost delta vs. original: high
- Anti-pattern check: pass only if capability access remains explicit; failure if one family roster silently shares everything.

## Job elevation

The original offer solves remote control. The expanded frame solves a more valuable family job:

> Establish a predictable agreement that children can understand and increasingly manage, caregivers can share without constant interruption, and devices can enforce without ambiguity.

This is not a candidate new top-level JTBD yet. It is a sharper expression of `jtbd-carry-intentions-into-action`, `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`, and `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life` within Maya's family-participation flow.

## Frame recommendation

**Run the design-thinking loop with an expanded frame.**

Name the concept **Family Access Agreements** for design purposes. Screen Time is the first enforcement surface, but the product frame is not “a chores app” or “a remote unlock app.” It is a family policy and trust system that turns real-life agreements into clear, progressively independent access.

The expansion is material enough to confirm before divergence. Phase 2 should compare at least three genuinely different operating models inside this frame rather than immediately specifying one earned-minutes implementation.
