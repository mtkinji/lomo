## When this job is hot

The user notices something they mean to do: call someone, follow up after an event, make a small move on a Goal, repair a relationship, or carry a Chapter insight forward. The intention is real, but fragile. If it depends on opening an app, creating the right object, choosing a time, remembering later, and logging the outcome, it may disappear.

## What "done" feels like for the user

The user can hand Kwilt an intention in the moment and trust it to carry the thread forward: capture it, prompt at a useful time, reduce the activation energy with a draft or concrete next step, and close the loop when it happens. They still feel like the author of their life; Kwilt is doing trusted follow-through support, not taking over.

## Notes

This is the demand-side home for Kwilt's most monetizable agentic moments. It sits under `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` because the outcome is real progress, but it is narrower than planning: the job is carrying intention across time, channels, prompts, action help, and loop closure.

Guardrails:

- Activities are the safest action surface for standing permission.
- Goal changes should usually be proposed and confirmed.
- Arcs should never be silently created or reshaped.
- External actions, such as sending messages to other people, should be draft-first until explicit consent patterns are proven.
