## When this job is hot

The user is being asked to put real, intimate things into Kwilt — identity directions, hopes, lived experience. That's a high-trust ask. If the app loses data, sends manipulative notifications, surprises with pricing, gamifies effort, or starts to feel like every other productivity app, trust collapses fast and is hard to win back.

## What "done" feels like for the user

The app feels calm, transparent, and intentional. Notifications respect their attention. Capture never loses data. AI suggestions feel like a thoughtful companion, not a salesperson. Pricing is honest, the data is portable, the language is humble. They don't have to be vigilant.
