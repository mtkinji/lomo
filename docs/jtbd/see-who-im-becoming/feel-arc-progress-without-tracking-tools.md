## When this job is hot

The user wants to feel that they're moving along their Arcs — that "becoming" isn't just a claim. But every progress-tracking system they've tried has either reduced identity to numbers (which falsifies it) or required so much input that it became another project competing for the time they wanted to spend *being* the person.

## What "done" feels like for the user

Looking at the app, there's a felt sense of motion on each Arc — recent Activities surface naturally, Force Actual aggregates without effort, the language describes movement rather than measurement ("trended toward Mastery this week" not "78% to goal"). The user doesn't have to *make* progress visible; the system reveals it from what they already captured.

## Anti-patterns

- Percentage progress bars on identity-anchored Goals.
- Streaks attached to Arcs.
- "Health scores" or composite indices that flatten the Forces.
- Anything that says "you're falling behind" — drift is information, not failure.

## Notes

The Force Intent vs Force Actual mismatch ([docs/life-architecture-model.md](../../life-architecture-model.md) §1) is the substrate for this JTBD. Mismatch is the *signal*; the system surfaces it as observation, not as judgment.

This JTBD intentionally rejects most "engagement metric" patterns. Engagement here is a side effect of the user feeling known, not a target.
