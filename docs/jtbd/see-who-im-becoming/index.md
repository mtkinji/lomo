## When this job is hot

The user senses there are directions of growth they care about — vocations, relationships, character — but those directions feel diffuse, hard to name, easy to lose track of when daily work crowds in. They want to keep the bigger story of who they're becoming present without it becoming another thing to manage.

## What "done" feels like for the user

When they look at Kwilt, they recognize themselves in their Arcs — the language feels accurate, not aspirational marketing. The next small thing they do today connects, without effort, to who they're trying to become. Drift is information, not failure.

## Notes

This is the *primary* personal-relevance JTBD for Kwilt. The four-object model in [docs/life-architecture-model.md](../../life-architecture-model.md) exists to serve this job at its core: Arcs name the directions of becoming, Goals translate them into months, Activities make them daily, Chapters reflect on whether they happened.

If a feature claims to serve this JTBD but produces *productivity-app affordances* (dashboards, streaks, completion percentages), it's probably misframed.
