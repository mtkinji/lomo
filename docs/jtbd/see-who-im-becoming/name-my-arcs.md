## When this job is hot

The user senses a few directions of growth they care about — vocations, character traits, relationships — but the language they have for those directions feels either too clinical ("Health"), too aspirational ("Become my best self"), or too borrowed from someone else's framework. They want language that lands the way their inner voice lands.

## What "done" feels like for the user

When they read their Arc names later, they recognize themselves. The names aren't fancy — sometimes they're plain ("Family Stewardship," "Becoming a Project Finisher") — but they're *theirs*. An outsider reading the list would learn something specific about who this person is trying to become.

## Anti-patterns

- Pre-baked "life-area" templates (Health / Career / Relationships) — these violate the JTBD by handing the user generic language.
- Heavy onboarding gating on Arc creation. The [arc-aspiration FTUE](../../arc-aspiration-ftue.md) is the gold-standard counter-example: tap-only, identity-ingredients-first, name synthesized from the answers.
- Productivity-app voice ("optimize," "level up," "crush it"). Kills resonance instantly.

## Notes

[docs/arc-aspiration-ftue.md](../../arc-aspiration-ftue.md) is the canonical implementation reference. This JTBD's success metric is qualitative — when a user sees their Arc name a month later, do they still recognize themselves in it?
