## When this job is hot

The user has more aspirations than time. Most apps make this worse by inviting more inputs, more lists, more tracking. They want a small number of Goals — the few that actually correspond to a season of life — and a sense that movement is happening on those, not on whichever items happened to feel urgent today.

## What "done" feels like for the user

A small set of Goals (per Arc) feel alive — not stale, not abandoned. Progress shows up as evidence of real work, not metric inflation. The user can name what they're working toward this season without consulting the app.

## Notes

The distinction between *identity-anchored Goals* and *outcome-based Goals* in [docs/life-architecture-model.md](../life-architecture-model.md) matters here. Outcome-based Goals tolerate metrics; identity-anchored Goals must not be reduced to numbers, or this JTBD is violated.

Force Intent vs Force Actual is a primary surface for "real progress" — when Actual diverges from Intent, the system has something genuine to surface, beyond raw counts.
