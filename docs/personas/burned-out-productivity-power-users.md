# Burned-out productivity power users

## Representative persona: Marcus

Marcus has tried Todoist, Notion, calendars, habits, AI chats, and a few bespoke systems. He is capable and motivated, but his tools keep turning into maintenance work. He does not need another place to dump tasks. He needs help deciding what deserves attention and keeping that connected to the life he says he wants.

## Why Kwilt Might Matter

Kwilt gives Marcus a structure that is not just more task management: Arcs for direction, Goals for the few live commitments, Activities for real motion, and Chapters for reflection.

## What Marcus Is Trying To Do

- Reduce system upkeep.
- Keep the few important commitments visible.
- Capture work without over-classifying it.
- Trust AI only when it is inspectable and reversible.

## What Would Make This Feel Wrong

- A generic productivity dashboard.
- More views without a reason to choose.
- AI that silently reorganizes his life model.
- Features that reward task volume instead of meaningful movement.

## Demand Hierarchy

Hero JTBD:
- `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - Marcus needs help deciding what deserves attention and making real progress without rebuilding another productivity system.

Supporting JTBDs:
- `jtbd-carry-intentions-into-action` - his problem often starts with good intentions that never become concrete action.
- `jtbd-capture-and-find-meaning` - capture has to be lighter than maintaining another system.
- `jtbd-make-sense-of-the-season` - reflection helps him see what mattered without combing through task history.
- `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life` - he will churn if Kwilt feels like another manipulative productivity tool.

Feature-level descent:
- Planning, recommendations, and goal limits descend from `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter`.
- Quick capture and retroactive alignment descend from `jtbd-capture-and-find-meaning`.
- AI operator, desktop, and bulk organization work must satisfy `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`.
