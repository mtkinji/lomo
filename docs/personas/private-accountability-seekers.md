# Private accountability seekers

## Representative persona: David

David wants someone in the loop on what he is trying to do, but he does not want his life turned into content. He might share a goal with a spouse, mentor, friend, coach, or small group, but only if the boundary is clear.

## Why Kwilt Might Matter

Kwilt can give David accountability without performance: chosen people, chosen goals, signals instead of surveillance, and no public feed.

## What David Is Trying To Do

- Make a commitment feel more real.
- Invite the right person into the right slice.
- Share enough to receive support.
- Keep private work private.

## What Would Make This Feel Wrong

- Default-public sharing.
- Leaderboards, feeds, or social pressure loops.
- Accidentally exposing raw activities or reflections.
- Accountability reminders that feel like nagging.

## Demand Hierarchy

Hero JTBD:
- `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in` - David needs support from the right people without exposing the wrong parts of his life.

Supporting JTBDs:

- `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - shared goals need to be concrete enough for accountability.

Feature-level descent:
- Shared goals, invitations, and check-ins descend from `jtbd-invite-the-right-people-in`.
- Visibility controls, permissions, and share previews descend from `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`.
- Accountability reminders descend from `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` when they help a real commitment move.
