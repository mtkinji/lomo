# People restarting after a hard season

## Representative persona: Elena

Elena had a system, a rhythm, or a set of intentions, and then life interrupted it. Maybe it was a move, illness, new baby, grief, burnout, job change, or just months of accumulated drift. Coming back is hard because most tools show her what she failed to maintain.

## Why Kwilt Might Matter

Kwilt can help Elena return without shame: notice what happened, make sense of the season, and choose a next step that fits the life she is actually in now.

## What Elena Is Trying To Do

- Re-enter without being punished by stale plans.
- Understand what changed.
- Keep what still matters.
- Take one honest next step.

## What Would Make This Feel Wrong

- Red badges, lost streaks, or "falling behind" copy.
- Requiring recommitment before she can dismiss something.
- Pretending the drift did not happen.
- Motivational cheerleading that feels fake.

## Demand Hierarchy

Hero JTBD:
- `jtbd-recover-when-i-drift-from-an-arc` - Elena needs a way back into her intentions that treats drift as information, not failure.

- `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life` - return surfaces have to feel safe, calm, and non-punitive.
- `jtbd-move-the-few-things-that-matter` - the next step has to be small enough to re-enter honestly.

Feature-level descent:
- Drift recovery, stale goal handling, and gentle nudges descend from `jtbd-recover-when-i-drift-from-an-arc`.
- Chapters and recap surfaces descend from `jtbd-make-sense-of-the-season`.
- Notification tone, dismissal, and recommitment flows must satisfy `jtbd-trust-this-app-with-my-life`.
